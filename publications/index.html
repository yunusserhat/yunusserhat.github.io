  2. Comparative Assessment of CNN and Transformer U-Nets in Multiple Sclerosis Lesion Segmentation
    Beytullah Sarica, Yunus Serhat Bicakci, and Dursun Zafer Seker
    International Journal of Imaging Systems and Technology, 2025
    e70146 IMA-25-143.R2
    Abs DOI Bib

    ABSTRACT Multiple sclerosis (MS) is a chronic autoimmune disease that causes lesions in the central nervous system. Accurate segmentation and quantification of these lesions are essential to monitor disease progression and evaluate treatments. Several architectures are used for such studies, the most popular being U-Net-based models. Therefore, this study compares CNN-based and Transformer-based U-Net architectures for MS lesion segmentation. Six U-Net architectures based on CNN and transformer, namely U-Net, R2U-Net, V-Net, Attention U-Net, TransUNet, and SwinUNet, were trained and evaluated on two MS datasets, ISBI2015 and MSSEG2016. T1-w, T2-w, and FLAIR sequences were jointly used to obtain more detailed features. A hybrid loss function, which involves the addition of focal Tversky and Dice losses, was exploited to improve the performance of models. This study was carried out in three steps. First, each model was trained separately and evaluated in each dataset. Second, each model was trained on the ISBI2015 dataset and evaluated on the MSSEG2016 dataset and vice versa. Finally, these two datasets were combined to increase the training samples and assessed on the ISBI2015 dataset. Accordingly, the R2U-Net and the V-Net models (CNN-based) achieved the best ISBI scores among the other models. The R2U-Net model achieved the best ISBI scores in the first and last steps with average scores of 92.82 and 92.91, while the V-Net model achieved the best ISBI score in the second step with an average score of 91.28. Our results show that CNN-based models surpass the Transformer-based U-Net models in most metrics for MS lesion segmentation.

  3. Big Data and Social Media Analytics: Opportunities for Interactive Crime Mapping
    Yunus Serhat Bicakci, Alina Ristea, and Kate Bowers
    2025
    Abs DOI Bib

    The mapping of crime has evolved and developed over the years in parallel with advancements in technology, exhibiting significant effectiveness in supporting crime prevention efforts. Crime mapping is predicted to evolve and increase further in effectiveness by encompassing not only advancements in geographical information technologies but also incorporating state-of-the-art in terms of advances in big data and artificial intelligence. In this chapter the opportunities for interactive crime mapping have been explored through the utilization of large data sets obtained from social media, mainly focusing on analysing this data using artificial intelligence applications, such as natural language processing. This new approach aims to add to the analytic toolkit available to aid crime prevention by facilitating the analysis of a large volume of data and enabling near real-time applicability in crime mapping with the assistance of cloud technologies.
